Good Lored

A fast, social lore-sharing game about truths, tall tales, and props.

Overview

Goal Earn points by telling the most compelling story that fits the prompt. The Lore Lord (judge) picks the round's best story. If your story is made up you earn 1 point; if it's a true story you earn 2 points. You also gain +1 point per prop you successfully weave into the story.

Back to Game

Quick Start

  1. Make a room and join eachother's games
  2. Each player tells a story (true or made up), weaving in up to 3 props.
  3. Lore Lord picks the best story → award points (1 made up, 2 true, +1 per prop).
  4. Rotate Lore Lord, refresh props, repeat.

Round Structure

  1. Reveal Prompt — The Lore Lord draws a prompt card and reads it aloud (e.g., “Share a piece of dating lore about yourself”).
  2. Storytelling — In turn order, each player tells a story inspired by the prompt. Stories may be true or made up. Try to naturally include any of your prop cards (words/objects). You may use from 0 to 3 props.
  3. Questions (optional) — After a story, players may ask up to 1–2 brief clarifying questions.
  4. Judging — The Lore Lord picks the best story based on fit to prompt, creativity, and delivery. The chosen storyteller earns base points and any prop bonuses (see Scoring).
  5. Truth Reveal — The chosen storyteller states whether the story was true or made up (no proof required; table trust applies).
  6. Rotate — Discard used props, deal 3 new prop cards to each player, and pass the Lore Lord role to the next player.

Scoring

ConditionPoints
Best story & made up1
Best story & true2
Each prop card woven in (0–3)+1 each

Only the Lore Lord’s chosen story scores for the round. Ties: the Lore Lord breaks ties; or award both and skip the next round’s judging to keep pace.

Judge Guidance

  • Reward stories that clearly match the prompt.
  • Favor truth when quality is close (the 2-point bonus encourages real sharing).
  • Props should feel integrated, not tacked on; obvious shoehorning may be ignored.
  • Keep the table moving — ~60–90 seconds per story.

House Rules & Variants

  • Group Vote: Everyone votes; Lore Lord breaks ties.
  • Detective Bonus: If the table unanimously misjudges truth/lie, award the storyteller +1 extra.

Sample Prompts

  • Share a piece of dating lore about yourself.
  • Tell a story about a time you got completely lost.
  • What’s your most unbelievable coincidence?
  • Describe a small decision that changed everything.

Sample Prop Cards

  • rubber duck · rooftop · a talk
  • casserole · voicemail · green shoes
  • forklift · choir · 3 a.m.
